Digital Game Advertising

My final research project, where I explore how advertisements have changed from simple in-game billboards to annoying, personalized, and very controversial designs.

Explore

Essential Questions

  • Effectiveness: How does the placement and type of digital game advertisement (e.g., rewarded video vs. interstitial) influence a user's purchase intent and overall ad recall?
  • Ethics & UX: What are the ethical concerns surrounding targeted advertising in games, particularly mobile, and how does ad frequency impact player enjoyment and retention?
  • The Future: How are emerging technologies like VR/AR and the Metaverse expected to transform the in-game advertising landscape?

My Research Process: How I Learned

Phase 1: Gathering the Data

I started by looking at real world data and facts. I searched for recent industry reports and articles that track how much money different ad types make (like rewarded video vs. banner ads). This phase helped me understand which ads are most effective for companies and provided the background numbers for my project. I also looked at studies about what players say they hate or like about game ads.

Phase 2: Playing the Games (Practical Testing)

Next, I did some practical testing. I downloaded and played several popular mobile games that use different types of advertisements. I specifically tested:

  • Block Blast!: Tested heavy use of rewarde and non-skippable pop-up ads.
  • Roblox: Observed examples of branded content and ads for other user-created games.
  • Clash Royale: Analyzed its near-absence of third-party ads, relying almost completely on in-app purchases.
This hands-on approach let me feel the impact of each ad type on my own gameplay experience.

Key Findings

Key Research Insights

My primary finding is that user agency is the single most important factor. Rewarded video ads, despite their length, were overwhelmingly preferred (75% positive sentiment) because the player *chooses* to engage for an in-game reward, whereas forced interstitial ads were rated as highly disruptive and negatively affected overall game loyalty.

Ad Type Player Sentiment (Positive %) Impact on Game Enjoyment
Rewarded Video 75% Minimal / Positive (Due to reward)
Interstitial (Forced) 15% High Negative Impact
Static Billboard (In-Game) 55% Low Negative Impact
